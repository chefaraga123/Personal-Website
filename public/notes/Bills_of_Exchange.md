# Bills of Exchange

A written legally binding order from one party (the drawer) to another party (the drawee) to pay a certain sum of money to a third party (the payee) at a certain place and time.

### Entities involved
- **The drawer** is the party that creates the bill of exchange, usually the party purchasing the goods or services.
- **The drawee** is the party that is obligated to pay the bill of exchange, usually the party selling the goods or services.
- **The payee** is the party that is entitled to receive the payment, usually the party lending the money.
